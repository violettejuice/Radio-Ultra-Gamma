GAMMA RAYS

Gamma rays are the most energetic, shortest-wavelength form of electromagnetic radiation. Produced by radioactive decay, nuclear explosions, and extreme cosmic events.

Science Nasa

FUN FACT

Supernovas and gamma-ray bursts can release more energy in seconds than the Sun will emit over its entire lifetime.

X-RAYS

X-rays are high-energy waves that can pass through soft tissues, allowing imaging of bones and internal structures.

Science Nasa

TELESCOPES

Earth's atmosphere blocks most X-rays from space, which is why space telescopes are needed to detect them.

ULTRAVIOLET

Ultraviolet is invisible light from the Sun with higher energy than visible light, responsible for both vitamin D production and sunburn.

Science Nasa

VISIBLE LIGHT

Visible light is the small portion of the electromagnetic spectrum that human eyes can detect.

INFRA-RED

Infra-red is invisible electromagnetic radiation with longer wavelengths than visible light, commonly experienced as heat emitted by warm objects and used for thermal imaging.

European Space Agency

FUN FACT

Several animals possess the ability to detect infrared light or thermal radiation, allowing them to sense heat from prey or their environment, often in total darkness.

MICROWAVES

Microwaves are higher-energy radio waves used in communication, radar, and cooking technologies.

Wikipedia

FUN FACT

The Cosmic Microwave Background is leftover radiation from the Big Bang, filling the entire universe.

RADIO WAVES

Radio waves are the longest-wavelength, lowest-energy part of the electromagnetic spectrum, commonly used to transmit information wirelessly.

Encyclopedia Britannica
